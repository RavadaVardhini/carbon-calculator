India Context
1.9
tonnes CO₂ per person per year (India average)
India's per-capita carbon footprint is among the lowest globally — but it's rising fast. Urban college students typically produce 1.5–3× the national average due to higher energy, transport, and food consumption.
🌳
Tree Equivalence
—
trees needed to absorb your annual emissions
One mature tree absorbs about 21 kg of CO₂ per year. Your footprint requires this many trees working all year just to balance what you emit — and India is still losing forest cover.
🌍
Global Budget
2.5T
tonnes — the safe global limit per person per year
To limit global warming to 1.5°C, each person on Earth must emit no more than 2.5 tonnes of CO₂ per year by 2030. Most developed countries emit 8–15 tonnes per person. The choices you make today shape that number.
🌡️
Why It Matters
+1.5°C
the tipping point scientists warn us about
Beyond 1.5°C of warming, we face more extreme floods, droughts, and heatwaves — especially in South Asia. Hyderabad and coastal Andhra are among the most climate-vulnerable regions in India. This isn't a future problem.
💡
5 Easy Actions For Students
Small changes, real impact
  • Switch to public transport or cycling — transport is often the #1 source of student emissions.
  • Eat one less meat meal per week — beef and chicken production emits 5–30× more CO₂ than vegetables.
  • Unplug chargers & switch off lights — idle electronics account for 10% of home electricity.
  • Carry a reusable bag and bottle — prevents hundreds of plastic items from landfills each year.
  • Reduce, then reuse, then recycle — buying less is always more impactful than recycling more.
