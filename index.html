Card 1 — What is Carbon Footprint?
CO₂
Carbon Dioxide — the main cause of global warming
A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases — especially CO₂ — produced by everything you do. Travel, food, electricity, shopping — all of it adds up. The bigger your footprint, the more heat gets trapped in our atmosphere.
🚗
Card 2 — Biggest Source of CO₂
~50%
of your footprint can come from transport alone
Petrol vehicles are one of the biggest sources of personal CO₂. A petrol two-wheeler emits about 110g of CO₂ per km. If you travel 10 km daily, that's over 400 kg of CO₂ every year — just getting to college. Walking, cycling, or taking the bus makes a huge difference.
🥗
Card 3 — Food and Your Footprint
5–30×
more CO₂ from meat compared to vegetables
The food on your plate has a carbon cost too. Producing 1 kg of beef creates up to 30 kg of CO₂, while 1 kg of vegetables creates less than 2 kg. A vegetarian diet is one of the single most effective ways to lower your personal carbon footprint.
🌡️
Card 4 — The Greenhouse Effect
+1.5°C
the dangerous warming limit scientists warn about
The greenhouse effect happens when gases like CO₂ trap heat in Earth's atmosphere — like a blanket around the Earth. The more CO₂ we emit, the thicker the blanket gets. Beyond 1.5°C of warming, we face extreme floods, droughts, and heatwaves — especially in South Asia.
☀️
Card 5 — Renewable Energy
0g
CO₂ emitted by solar, wind, or water energy
Renewable energy comes from sources that never run out — sun, wind, and water. Unlike coal or petrol, they produce zero CO₂ when generating electricity. India is now the 4th largest solar energy producer in the world. Switching to renewables is key to fighting climate change.
♻️
Card 6 — Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
3R
the golden rule of sustainable living
The 3Rs — Reduce, Reuse, Recycle — help cut down waste and CO₂. Reduce: buy only what you need. Reuse: use things again instead of throwing them. Recycle: turn waste into something new. In that order — reducing is always more powerful than recycling.
